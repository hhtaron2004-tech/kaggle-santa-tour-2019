## üß† Solution Approach

This solver uses a two-stage optimization strategy:

1Ô∏è‚É£ Greedy Capacity-Aware Assignment  
- Families are sorted by size in descending order, as large families are harder to schedule.  
- Each family is assigned to their best available preferred day (choice_0 ‚Üí choice_9) without exceeding daily capacity.  
- Families that remain unassigned are then placed in days with low occupancy to ensure all days meet minimum and maximum limits.  
This produces a strong initial solution where most families get one of their top choices.

2Ô∏è‚É£ Local Search Optimization  
- After the greedy schedule is built, the algorithm iterates over each family and each of their 10 preferred days.  
- If moving a family to a different preferred day reduces the total Kaggle cost, the change is kept.  
- This hill-climbing process gradually improves preference satisfaction and reduces accounting penalties, producing a locally optimized solution while keeping constraints satisfied.

## üßÆ Cost Function

The project fully implements the official Kaggle scoring formula:

- Preference Penalty  
  Families incur penalties based on which of their 10 choices they are assigned to. The farther from the top choice, the higher the penalty.

- Daily Occupancy Constraints  
  Each day must have between 125 and 300 visitors. Violations are heavily penalized.

- Accounting Penalty  
  Penalizes large day-to-day attendance fluctuations to encourage smooth schedules.

The total cost is the sum of all three components, and the optimizer minimizes this value.

## ‚öôÔ∏è Workflow

1. Load the family preference and size dataset.  
2. Sort families by size.  
3. Assign families greedily to preferred days while respecting capacity limits.  
4. Place any unassigned families into low-occupancy days.  
5. Evaluate the schedule using the Kaggle cost function.  
6. Apply local search to further optimize assignments.  
7. Save the final optimized schedule as submission_<score>.csv.
